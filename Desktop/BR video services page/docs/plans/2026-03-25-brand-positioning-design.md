# Brand & Positioning Page Design
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**File to create:** `brand-positioning.html`

## Overview
A service page for Branch Road's Brand & Positioning offering. Follows the design language and section structure established by `demand-generation.html` — three service area sections (not four), each with a headline, copy, output pills, and an example card. No separate "problem" section; the tensions are baked into the copy.

## Core Tensions (woven into copy, not stated explicitly)
1. Brand investment that doesn't move the needle — spend without commercial impact
2. Brand investment without knowing who it's aimed at — missing the target through lack of strategy and research

## Branch Road Differentiator
The sequence matters: understand the market first → build the narrative → wire it into go-to-market. Research leads, not aesthetics. Brand output connects directly to GTM so it gets used, not just approved.

## Section Structure

### Section 2 — Journey audit (includes audience/competitor analysis)
- **Headline:** Know where you stand before you move
- **Copy:** Most brand work starts with a blank page. We start with a thorough read of where you are — your current positioning, your audience, your competitors, and the gaps between them. So the narrative we build is grounded in reality, not assumption.
- **Output pills:** Positioning audit · Audience mapping · Competitor analysis · Message gap assessment · Stakeholder interviews
- **Example card:** Positioning audit — a structured read of where your brand sits relative to competitors, what's working, what's not, and where the white space is.

### Section 3 — Brand narrative & messaging
- **Headline:** A story that earns its place in market
- **Copy:** We build brand narratives around a clear, defensible position — not a list of features dressed up as values. Copy that speaks to the right level, in the right voice, with a point of view buyers actually remember.
- **Output pills:** Brand narrative · Messaging framework · Value proposition · Tone of voice · Website copy · Campaign messaging
- **Example card:** Messaging framework — a single document that aligns sales, marketing, and leadership on what you say, how you say it, and who you're saying it to.

### Section 4 — GTM
- **Headline:** Built to be used, not just approved
- **Copy:** Brand work that stays in a deck doesn't move the needle. We wire positioning into your go-to-market — so your sales team, your content, and your campaigns all pull in the same direction from day one.
- **Output pills:** GTM playbook · Sales messaging · Launch content · Campaign framework · Channel strategy
- **Example card:** GTM playbook — the bridge between brand narrative and execution. What to say in each channel, to each audience, at each stage.

### Section 5 — CTA
- **Headline:** Ready to build a brand that knows what it's for?
- **Sub-copy:** Tell us where you are. We'll start with what we'd need to understand before writing a word.
- Contact link + back to services link
