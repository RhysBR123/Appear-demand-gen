Get the foundations wrong
and nothing else matters.

Most demand gen programmes fail before launch. Not because the ads are bad — because nobody agreed on what a good lead looks like, Sales weren't in the room, and there's no system for learning what's working.

This is what separates pipeline from PowerPoint.

A clearly defined ICP — who you're selling to, what a good lead looks like, agreed MQL and SQL definitions. If Marketing and Sales are working off different definitions, you're already losing.

Sales buy-in on messaging — not a courtesy CC on the brief. Sales need to be equipped to use what Marketing puts into market, or it dies on arrival.

Marketing and Sales alignment — shared framework, clean handoff, SLA on lead response time. No grey areas.

Lead scoring — so Sales sees leads ready for a conversation, not everyone who downloaded a PDF.

A feedback loop on lead quality — structured input from Sales on what's converting, so targeting sharpens over time instead of stagnating.

Tracking and reporting infrastructure — UTM tagging, campaign attribution, closed-loop reporting. If you can't trace a closed deal back to the campaign that sourced it, you're guessing.

Dedicated optimisation resource — campaigns don't run themselves. Someone watches the numbers and pulls the levers, every week.

Budget and headroom to iterate — especially before September. The first version of anything is never the best.

Recommendations

Six things to get right
before September.

Not a wish list. A priority sequence.

01
Refresh your Book-of-Business lists by region

Work with Sales leaders to update BoB lists across North America (including Canada), LATAM, EMEA, and APAC. These are the targeting foundation for everything. If they're stale, every dollar you spend on ads is aimed at the wrong person.

02
Lock in your ICP — then hunt lookalikes

Agree the Ideal Customer Profile, then use it to find lookalike companies and run always-on awareness against them. This builds a constant stream of opportunities Sales doesn't have on their radar yet — across every region.

Knowing who you're targeting is half the job. Knowing where to reach them is the other half. LinkedIn is the natural home for Appear's audience, but the best programmes don't live on one channel. Do the research — your prospects are somewhere you haven't looked yet.

03
Define your top personas and tailor everything

Pick the two or three that matter most — Operations vs. Engineering, for example — and build distinct creative, landing pages, and nurture sequences for each. Specificity converts. Generic messaging doesn't.

04
Audit what you already have

Before building anything new, take stock. A full content audit across campaigns, sales assets, videos, and collateral will surface material you can repurpose, refresh, or repackage. Saves budget, compresses your timeline.

05
Run always-on awareness — not just seasonal surges

NAB matters. Events matter. But they can't carry a programme alone. Always-on awareness does three things seasonal campaigns can't: steady top-of-funnel flow, pre-loaded retargeting pools before big pushes, and momentum through the dead zones — including Q5, when most competitors go quiet.

06
Build a campaign playbook for Sales

One page per campaign: which ads are running, key messages, follow-up templates. Takes an afternoon to create, transforms the Marketing-to-Sales handoff. Sales follows up with confidence from day one because they know exactly what the prospect just saw.
